Clearing the Confusion

Common Myths About Nonprofits & the 508(c)(1)(A)

Myth 1

All Nonprofits Must File for 501(c)(3) Status

False. The 508(c)(1)(A) is automatically tax-exempt. No IRS application or approval letter is required.

Myth 2

508(c)(1)(A) Organizations Are Secretive or Illegal

False. These are federally recognized, legally established organizations protected under the U.S. tax code and First Amendment.

Myth 3

Churches Must Register with the IRS to Be Tax-Exempt

False. Under 508(c)(1)(A), churches and integrated auxiliaries are automatically exempt from this requirement.

Myth 4

All Donations Are Automatically Tax-Deductible

Not always. Purchases are not deductible. Pure donations with nothing given in return may be. Consult your tax advisor.

Myth 5

Nonprofits Can't Earn Income or Own Property

False. A 508(c)(1)(A) can earn income, own property, and open a bank account while maintaining its exempt status.
